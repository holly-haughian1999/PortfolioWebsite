Problem

We had to create a type specimen screen based on a favoured typeface, I chose Palatino for its strong and graceful lettering, it's highly legible and has an open style. I wasnâ€™t aware of how important the typeface in a project is before this project, it opened my eyes to the impact a font can have.

Role: Design, Typography.

Tools: Pencil and paper, Adobe Illustrator.

Process

I began this project by researching Palatino and learning its history so I could truly appreciate the font, I also studied other typescreens and their colour/ layout. Then I was able to take pencil to paper and begin sketching.



I digitised three concepts and realised I wasn't fully happy with them once designed, feedback sessions also helped me to improve my design process. I then sketched three new designs as shown below, I wanted to use bold colours to match the strong lettering style but without taking away from the typeface. I found it challenging to feature all the elements I wanted to without overcrowding the design, however as I simplified it I was able to overcome this challenge.




Below is the finished type specimen screen.


Reflections

Completing this project enabled me to appreciate typography and see how big of an impact it has in the design industry. I went for simple, bold colours to compliment but not hinder the typeface. I also included information regarding Palatino to allow viewers to gain knowledge on it. I'm pleased with the outcome as I found it challenging to find a design that I thought visually worked well, I plan on looking into typography more to help me understand using it in design better.
